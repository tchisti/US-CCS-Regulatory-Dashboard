# North American CCS Regulatory Guide

**Comprehensive interactive guide to Carbon Capture & Storage (CCS) regulations across Canada and the United States.**

📄 **[View Live Dashboard →](https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/north-american-ccs-regulatory-guide/)**

## Coverage

### 🇨🇦 Canada (4 Provinces)
- **Alberta** — Advanced framework, AER-regulated, Crown pore space tenure, Post-Closure Stewardship Fund
- **Saskatchewan** — World leader in CO₂-EOR, Boundary Dam 3, Ministerial Orders
- **British Columbia** — BCER expanded jurisdiction (2022), PNGA Part 14 storage licences
- **Ontario** — Emerging: prohibition removed (2023), special projects enabled (2024)

### 🇺🇸 United States (6 States)
- **Texas** — Class VI primacy granted Dec 2025, RRC administration
- **Louisiana** — Class VI primacy Jan 2024, Oct 2025 moratorium on new apps
- **California** — EPA-administered, SB 905 framework, LCFS CCS Protocol, pipeline moratorium
- **Illinois** — SAFE CCS Act dual permitting, 2-year pipeline moratorium
- **North Dakota** — First primacy state (2018), 4 approved facilities, liability transfer
- **South Dakota** — Eminent domain ban (Mar 2025), Summit pipeline denied

## Features
- **195+ regulations** catalogued with direct links to official sources
- **Dual navigation**: by CCS category (Capture / Pipeline / Sequestration) and by regulatory level (Provincial/State / Federal)
- **Country switcher**: seamless Canada ↔ United States toggle
- **Search**: full-text search across all regulations
- **Federal incentive panels**: Canada CCUS ITC (50-60%) and US Section 45Q ($85-180/ton)
- **Responsive**: works on desktop, tablet, and mobile

## Deployment
